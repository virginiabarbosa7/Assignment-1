Historical Brazilian Elections in 2018

October 2018 will mark the tightest Presidential election in Brazil since redemocratization in 1989

Many candidates, few with real chances

The Brazilian elections has two rounds in October, one in the beginning and another in the end of the month.

There are currently 13 candidates in the Brazilian Presidential elections, and polls have shown that only two of them have real chances.

One is an extreme-right wing deputy, Jair Bolsonaro, which some speculate he can bring back an authoritarian regime. The other, Fernando Haddad, is a left-wing politician that was part of the previous government which led Brazil to its current financial crisis.

Population still in doubt

Although the 1st election round is right around the corner, the Brazilian people is still unsure who will best represent them.

According to the latest polls, if there is a 2nd round between Bolsonaro and Haddad, Bolsonaro would win.

However, Bolsonaro is rejected by 60% of the population according to polls.

Decision will be made October 6th

Brazillians are still uncertain onn who they will vote for.

According to analysts, its possible that voters will decide whom they will vote for one day before the election.
